My Work

The Kindness Project account management mobile application.

The kindness project is a nonprofit organization that supports foster families in Lehigh Valley and surrounding areas in Pennsylvania. They support foster families by providing free clothing, furniture, and essential items that these foster children may not otherwise have, or foster parents may not have the money to provide for them.

Problem Statement

We needed to design a mobile application to allow account management for foster parents to manage their accounts and documents right from their mobile device once their application is approved.

Audience

The audience for the Kindness Project mobile application is the foster parents and a streamedlined process for the Kindness Project staff.

Role

UX Designer

- Synthesized requests from stakeholder interviews

- Conducted competitive analysis

- Developed the User Persona

- Facilitated the Prioritization Matrix Session

- Crafted the Problem Statement

- Assisted with creating micro-interactions for the high-fidelity prototype

Constraints

Before meeting with our client, we understood we were redesigning the client's website. However, after speaking with our stakeholders, we needed to pivot and understand the request for a mobile app and a long list of requested features.

Due to the short timeline, we needed to understand the level of effort for the requests and the most important items we could accomplish for the client that meets the user's need while providing a quality product and then add additional features in phases.

Process

Our process for this project was simple.

We conducted a detailed competitor analysis and interviewed our stakeholders during the research phase.

We were able to develop the User Persona and define our Problem Statement from work completed during the research phase.

The Feature Prioritization Matrix helps us define the features to build now and determine our next steps as we add new features in stages.

From our findings, we could develop a solid mobile application to meet the stakeholders' requests while focusing on the user experience.

Lessons Learned

We learned from this process that instead of relying so heavily on the stakeholders for information, we could have found a group of users to interview.

It is essential to understand the design process is not 1, 2, or 3 and that you use the different tools based on the project scenario. It required me to understand other research elements to keep the project moving forward without having all the information.
